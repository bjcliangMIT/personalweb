I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economic Sociology at MIT Sloan School of Management. My work is aimed at management and organizations audiences, using formal modeling, computational methods, and field-informed inquiry to explain how group dynamics, governance processes, and digital infrastructures shape organizational and market outcomes.

I am especially interested in organization theory, collective action, strategic interaction, platform governance, and the measurement of social processes at scale.

Research

Projects and agenda

Job market paper

From Opinion Dynamics to Collective Action

Examines how asymmetric tolerance can transform disagreement into durable group polarization, linking micro-level opinion dynamics to collective action outcomes with implications for organizations, strategic alignment, and collective behavior. Supervised by Ezra Zuckerman and Ray Reagans at MIT.

Presented at PaCSS 2022, ASA 2023, and INAS 2023

Emerging dissertation direction

Data-Making Fans and Platform Governance

Uses a system dynamics perspective to study how fan labor, informal data practices, and platform governance co-evolve in digitally mediated communities, with broader implications for platform strategy, governance design, and organizational adaptation.

Presented at ASA 2025, INAS 2025, EGOS 2025, and the International System Dynamics Conference 2025

Working paper

From Composition to Conduct in the Boardroom

A dynamic NK model of board behavior. I designed the model in collaboration with Takahiro Inada, bringing simulation methods to classic business school questions about board composition, conduct, and decision-making.
