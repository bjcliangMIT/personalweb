Research on organizations, strategy, and platform governance.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economic Sociology at MIT Sloan School of Management and an MIT Presidential Graduate Fellow. I study how individual-level interactions affect organization-level outcomes, especially in settings like political polarization and platform governance.

My work combines formal modeling (e.g., agent-based modeling, system dynamics, network analysis, natural language processing) and qualitative research (e.g., interviews and participatory observation) for management and sociology audiences.

Supervised by Professors Ezra Zuckerman Sivan and Ray Reagans at MIT.

Research

Projects and agenda

Job market paper

Data-Making Fans and Platform Governance

Studies how fan labor, informal data practices, and platform governance co-evolve in digitally mediated communities, with implications for platform strategy, governance design, and organizational adaptation. Joint work with Vicky Chuqiao Yang.

Presented at ASA 2025, INAS 2025, EGOS 2025, and the International System Dynamics Conference 2025

Candidacy paper

From Opinion Dynamics to Collective Action

Examines how asymmetric tolerance can turn disagreement into durable polarization, linking micro-level opinion dynamics to collective action.

Presented at PaCSS 2022, ASA 2023, and INAS 2023

Working paper

From Composition to Conduct in the Boardroom

A dynamic NK model of board behavior, bringing simulation methods to questions of board composition, conduct, and decision-making. Joint work with INADA Takahiro.
